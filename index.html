Abstract

Pretraining navigation diffusion policies rely on large-scale expert demonstrations. These data are typically generated by a fully-informed oracle planner suited to a single nominal robot. This limits the policy's generalization to diverse embodiments and challenging scenarios, such as escaping dead ends or detouring long obstacles, that demand diverse local reactive behaviors with only onboard local observations.

Post-training the policy with reinforcement learning offers a principled remedy. However, previous RL for diffusion approaches lead to only marginal improvements because the intractable likelihood of diffusion policies renders policy gradients unstable, in addition to inefficient policy exploration. We propose a data-efficient diffusion RL post-training framework, GQRM (Group Q-score Reweighted Matching), for fine-tuning navigation diffusion policies.

GQRM introduces two complementary designs: a self-bootstrapped exploration strategy with behavior perturbation that preserves the pretrained policy prior, and a group Q-score normalization mechanism that computes per-trajectory values on each state for efficient reweighted score matching. By conducting distributed online RL training across heterogeneous embodiments, the resulting fine-tuned policy, X-NavDP, achieves state-of-the-art cross-embodiment visual navigation performance.

Generalist Navigation Policy

X-NavDP starts from a pretrained visual navigation diffusion policy and post-trains it with online reinforcement learning across heterogeneous robot embodiments. The goal is to retain the useful prior from demonstrations while expanding the policy's local reactive behaviors in hard navigation cases.

Results

X-NavDP improves the policy's ability to navigate novel behavior modes and robot embodiments, especially in hard cases that require dead-end recovery, long-obstacle detours, and onboard-observation-only reactions.

Key Contributions

Data-efficient diffusion RL post-training. GQRM improves navigation diffusion policies without requiring new oracle-generated expert demonstrations for every embodiment.

Stable reweighted score matching. Group Q-score normalization avoids directly depending on intractable diffusion policy likelihoods.

Cross-embodiment generalization. Distributed online training across heterogeneous robots produces a fine-tuned policy that transfers to novel embodiments and hard navigation behaviors.
